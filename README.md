# CS-250 Portfolio

## Sprint Review and Retrospective

For this portfolio, I chose my Sprint Review and Retrospective from the SNHU Travel project. This project allowed me to apply Agile and Scrum practices by taking on different Scrum roles, writing user stories, creating test cases, and reflecting on the software development process. It gave me a better understanding of how collaboration and continuous feedback contribute to building successful software.

### How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?

I interpret user needs by gathering requirements and breaking them into user stories. User stories help me understand what the user wants from their point of view, making it easier to develop features that provide value. They also give the development team clear goals and acceptance criteria.

### How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I like developing software in smaller steps instead of trying to complete everything at once. Agile showed me the value of planning, testing, receiving feedback, and making improvements throughout development. In future projects, I hope to continue using sprint planning, user stories, regular communication, and retrospectives to stay organized and adapt to changing requirements.

### What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member means communicating openly, being dependable, respecting other people's ideas, and being willing to help when needed. Every member of the Scrum Team has an important role, and working together helps produce better software and solve problems more effectively.
